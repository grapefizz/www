Plants vs. Zombies is my favorite game of all time, and seeing it slowly die out due to corporate greed is a shame. But it wasn't always like
this.

## the start
Pvz, develped and published by Popcap on May 5, 2009, started with humble beginings. It was a game made by a small team of developers and designers, only 4
people total. Everyone at Popcap had a passion for what they were working on, and they worked hard to bring Pvz to life. The composer, Laura
Shigihara, composed one of the most brilliant soundtracks in video game history. The game's lead designer, George Fan, created one of the most
versetile gameplay loops. Pvz is a massively successful game in part because of its very low skill floor and very high skill ceiling. Another
contributing factor is how widespread the game was, you could play the original pvz on just about any platform, every phone brand, pc and
console under the sun had a port of pvz. All of these factors combined made pvz the most downloaded game of 2010.

With all of this success and popularity, Popcap was bought out by EA in July of 2011 and shortly after that, development of Plants vs. Zombies
2 was started. Not much is known about the early stages of pvz 2's development, but from the start EA's influence was very apparent.

## the early cracks
With the release of pvz 2, players were shocked with the decisions made. The game launched with microtransactions, something that the first pvz
lacked entirely. The microtransactions in question were purchasable plants that could be bought for the low low price of only $5 each.
As the game went about it's life, with each update, EA's greed would show more and more. Each update brought more purchasable plants and even
new game modes that required a new currency to play.
